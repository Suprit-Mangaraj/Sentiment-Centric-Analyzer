**Sentiment-Centric Analyzer** is a data science and Natural Language Processing (NLP) project developed to analyze customer feedback and evaluate overall brand perception. The objective of the project was to transform large volumes of customer reviews into meaningful insights that could help organizations understand customer satisfaction levels and improve decision-making.

The project utilized customer review datasets stored in Excel files. The collected data first underwent preprocessing and cleaning, including handling missing values, removing irrelevant information, and preparing textual data for analysis. Various Python libraries were used for data manipulation, visualization, and text processing.

After preprocessing, NLP techniques were applied to extract sentiment information from customer reviews. The system classified reviews into positive, negative, and neutral categories, enabling a structured understanding of customer opinions. The analyzed results were then used to identify common trends, measure overall customer sentiment, and provide insights into the company's brand reputation.

The project also involved exploratory data analysis and visualization to present sentiment distribution and customer feedback patterns in an easily understandable format. These visual insights helped highlight areas where the company performed well and areas that required improvement.

Through this project, I gained practical experience in data cleaning, feature extraction, sentiment analysis, machine learning fundamentals, and working with real-world datasets. The project strengthened my understanding of how data science and NLP techniques can be applied to solve business problems by converting unstructured textual data into actionable insights.
